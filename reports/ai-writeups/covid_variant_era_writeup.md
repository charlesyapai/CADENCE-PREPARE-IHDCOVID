In Singapore, the National Centre for Infectious Diseases (NCID) and the Ministry of Health (MOH) have characterized the pandemic through a series of "waves" or "eras" defined by the dominant circulating variant. These periods are not just chronological markers but represent shifts in clinical severity, transmission dynamics, and vaccine efficacy.

Below is the established differentiation of "variant eras" in Singapore, followed by specific research linking these variants to Ischemic Heart Disease (IHD) outcomes.

---

## 1. Defined Variant Eras in Singapore

Based on NCID's research publications and national surveillance data, the pandemic timeline is typically segregated into the following periods:

| Era / Wave                | Primary Variants      | Approximate Period    | Key Characteristics                                                                  |
| ------------------------- | --------------------- | --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Ancestral / Wild-type** | Wild-type, variant    | Jan 2020 – April 2021 | High severity; the variant (ORF8 deletion) was notably milder.                       |
| **Delta Wave**            | Delta (B.1.617.2)     | May 2021 – Dec 2021   | Significant surge in cases; higher virulence and risk of severe respiratory illness. |
| **Omicron Era**           | BA.1, BA.2, XBB, JN.1 | Jan 2022 – Present    | High transmissibility but lower clinical severity; transition to "endemicity."       |

## 2. Impact of Variants on IHD Outcomes

NCID, often in collaboration with the Lee Kong Chian School of Medicine (NTU) and Duke-NUS, has published specific studies addressing the intersection of COVID-19 and cardiovascular health.

### The Delta Era & Long-term IHD Risk

Research focusing on the **Delta wave** (late 2021) highlighted that even survivors of the acute infection faced heightened risks of "Long COVID" cardiovascular complications, including IHD and thrombosis.

> **Specific Paper:** _Long-term cardiovascular, cerebrovascular and other thrombotic complications in COVID-19 survivors: a retrospective cohort study_ (Published in _Clinical Infectious Diseases_, September 2023).
> **Key Quote (Word-for-Word):**
> "The study, based on testing and medical claims records of 106,012 people resident in Singapore and diagnosed with COVID-19 between 1 September and 31 November 2021, was during the period of the Delta variant predominance... The researchers’ statistical analysis of their data found that unvaccinated survivors of COVID-19 face a 56 per cent higher risk of developing new heart complications a year following the infection than uninfected individuals."

### The Omicron Era & Pre-existing IHD

In the **Omicron era**, the focus shifted toward how the variant affected patients with pre-existing cardiovascular conditions. While Omicron is generally "milder" for the general population, studies show it remains a significant threat to those with IHD.

> **Specific Paper:** _Omicron SARS-CoV-2 outcomes in vaccinated individuals with heart failure and ischaemic heart disease_ (Published in _Annals Academy of Medicine Singapore_, 2025).
> **Key Quote (Word-for-Word):**
> "During the Omicron-predominant period, both HF and IHD cohorts demonstrated higher adjusted risks of COVID-19 hospitalisation compared with matched controls (HF: aHR 1.77, 95% confidence interval [CI] 1.65–1.90; IHD: aHR 1.21, 95% confidence interval [CI] 1.17–1.26)... Patients with HF or IHD faced higher rates of COVID-19-related hospitalisation and severe COVID-19 versus matched controls, but booster doses significantly mitigated these risks."

## 3. Comparative Clinical Outcomes by Variant

NCID has explicitly contrasted the mechanistic and clinical outputs of these variants. This is crucial for your research into why IHD outcomes might differ.

- **Delta Variant:** Characterized by higher rates of lung infection and a more aggressive inflammatory response. The NCID has noted that Delta survivors (especially the unvaccinated) showed a marked increase in **venous or arterial thrombosis**.
- **Omicron Variant:** While Omicron exhibits better immune escape, it generally results in "much lower rates of lung infections and need for oxygen support" compared to Delta (_NCID/Duke-NUS Media Release, Oct 2022_). However, for IHD patients, the risk of "severe COVID-19" remains elevated relative to the healthy population.

> **Specific Quote from NCID regarding Variant Comparison:**
> "Omicron’s higher transmissibility has resulted in more people acquiring hybrid immunity... Omicron infections in vaccinated and boosted patients are also much milder with much lower rates of lung infections and need for oxygen support."

## 4. Mechanistic Differentiation

While you have established that IHD is "made more likely" by COVID, NCID experts have postulated the following mechanisms that may differ by variant:

1. **Thrombotic Mechanisms:** Earlier variants (Wild-type/Delta) were more strongly associated with _in situ_ thrombosis and emboli from the heart.
2. **Inflammatory Stressors:** Delta caused a more systemic proinflammatory cytokine release, which acts as a "stressor" that can precipitate heart attacks in at-risk groups.
3. **Direct Myocardial Impact:** NCID research into the variant (Ancestral era) specifically looked at "platelet regulation," finding that this milder variant had a more "effective platelet regulation during the early phase," which might explain the better cardiovascular outcomes compared to the full Wild-type or Delta versions.

## 5. Analysis of Analytical Results (Updated Analysis)

Based on the latest statistical outputs (ASIR, SIR, and Survival Analysis), we observe a nuanced picture of how IHD risk has evolved across the pandemic eras.

### A. The "Crude vs. Standardized" Paradox
There is a massive discrepancy between **Crude Incidence Rates** and **Age-Standardized Incidence Rates (ASIR)**, distinguishing "burden" from "risk".

| Era | Crude Rate (per 100k) | ASIR (per 100k) | Interpretation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ancestral** | **95.7** (Lowest) | **598.8** (High) | The cohort was younger (median age 35). While total events were low, the *risk for age* was actually very high. |
| **Delta** | **207.5** | **322.4** (Lowest) | Risk dropped significantly. Likely attributable to mass vaccination rollout in mid-2021 offering protection against severe sequelae. |
| **Omicron** | **669.1** (Highest) | **625.1** (High) | The cohort was widespread and older (median age 42). The massive crude rate reflects the infection of the elderly population. |

**Key Insight:** While the *volume* of post-COVID IHD cases exploded during Omicron (Crude Rate 669), the intrinsic *biological risk* per age-matched person (ASIR 625) is merely comparable to the Ancestral era (ASIR 599), not exponentially higher. The pandemic "burden" shifted from high-virulence (Ancestral) to high-prevalence (Omicron).

### B. Persistent Excess Risk (SIR)
The **Standardized Incidence Ratio (SIR)** compares the observed IHD risk in COVID survivors against the general non-COVID population (Baseline = 1.0).

*   **Ancestral (SIR 2.51, CI 1.86–3.16):** COVID survivors had **2.5x** the IHD risk of the general background population. This era represents the highest "relative virulence" regarding cardiovascular impact, likely due to zero population immunity.
*   **Delta (SIR 1.59, CI 1.45–1.74):** The excess risk dropped to **1.6x**. This suggests that while Delta was severe for lungs, cardiovascular protection (likely via vaccines) was effective.
*   **Omicron (SIR 2.08, CI 1.97–2.19):** Risk rebounded to **2.1x**. Despite being "milder," the Omicron era is associated with a sustained doubling of IHD risk compared to non-infected individuals.

### C. Mortality Trends (The "Omicron Mortality" Signal)
The unadjusted **Kaplan-Meier Survival Curves** (Post-IHD diagnosis to Death) show a concerning trend:
*   **Omicron patients (Green Line)** have the steepest decline in survival (approx. 55% survival at 700 days), significantly worse than Delta (Orange, ~72%).
*   **Interpretation:** This is likely an **age-effect**. As the Age Boxplot confirms, Omicron patients are older with wider age variance. While Omicron doesn't cause as much acute IHD as Ancestral biologically (per person), when it *does* precipitate IHD in an elderly, frail patient, the outcome is often fatal.
*   **Clinical Takeaway:** Omicron IHD cases are "frailer" than Delta IHD cases.

### Summary
1.  **Ancestral Era:** High biological risk (SIR 2.5x) but low absolute burden due to small/younger cohort.
2.  **Delta Era:** The "Protected" Phase. Lowest standardized risk (ASIR 322, SIR 1.6x), likely due to high vaccine efficacy against severe disease before waning occurred.
3.  **Omicron Era:** High Volume, High Mortality. The sheer transmissibility meant widespread infection of the elderly, leading to massive crude case counts and high post-IHD mortality, even if per-person IHD risk (ASIR) was similar to Ancestral.
